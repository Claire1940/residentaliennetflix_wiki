<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Casting Journey and Production History">

The process of finding the right **Resident Alien actors** was critical for series creator Chris Sheridan. Adapting a comic book requires a delicate balance between respecting the source material and allowing actors to bring their own interpretations to the roles. The production, which primarily took place in British Columbia, Canada, utilized the stunning mountain landscapes to stand in for the fictional Patience, Colorado.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Pilot Development">
    Syfy ordered the pilot in 2018, with Chris Sheridan leading the adaptation of Peter Hogan and Steve Parkhouse's comic. The search for Harry was paramount, as the role required a unique blend of physical comedy and dramatic depth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assembling the Ensemble">
    In late 2018, Alan Tudyk was cast as the lead, followed quickly by Sara Tomko and Corey Reynolds. The producers looked for actors who could handle the show's "dark comedy" tone without losing the audience's sympathy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Principal Photography">
    Filming began in September 2020 in Delta, British Columbia. The production team used practical effects for Harry's alien form in later seasons, moving away from the purely prosthetic-heavy approach of the first season.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Series Expansion and Renewal">
    Following a successful Season 1 premiere in 2021, the show was renewed multiple times, eventually moving to a simulcast on USA Network for its final season in 2025 to reach a broader audience.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The production faced challenges, including shifting release dates and the logistical hurdles of filming in remote Canadian locations. However, the dedication of the cast and crew ensured that the show maintained its high quality, resulting in a 94% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes for its debut season.

<Callout type="info" title="Production Fact">
The alien language featured in the show is actually Esperanto, written in a custom right-to-left script. This attention to detail is a hallmark of the production's commitment to world-building.
</Callout>
