<Section icon={Shield} title="The Alien's Journey: A Moral Dilemma">
*Resident Alien* delves deep into the moral quandaries faced by Harry as he spends more time among humans. Initially, his mission is clear: destroy humanity to save Earth. However, his involuntary development of human emotions and his growing connections with Asta and others force him to question the morality of his directive.

  <Step num="1" title="Crash-Landing and Identity Theft">
    Harry's ship crash-lands, leading him to kill Dr. Harry Vanderspeigle and assume his identity in the small town of Patience, Colorado. He quickly finds himself embroiled in a local murder mystery.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Observing Humanity">
    As the town's new doctor, Harry is thrust into daily interactions with humans. He learns about their customs, emotions, and complexities, often with hilarious and awkward results, frequently narrating his observations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Developing Empathy">
    Through his relationships, particularly with Asta and Max, Harry begins to feel emotions he previously couldn't comprehend. This empathy directly conflicts with his mission, causing internal struggle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defending Humanity">
    Harry eventually shifts from his destructive mission to actively protecting humanity from other extraterrestrial threats, showcasing his complete moral transformation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Harry possesses superhuman strength, durability, agility, advanced intelligence, and shapeshifting abilities. He can also alter or remove human memories, making his disguise almost undetectable to most.

This journey of self-discovery and moral reckoning is a core theme of the series, making it more than just a sci-fi comedy. It explores what it means to be human from an outsider's perspective.
