<Section icon={Swords} title="Violence, Gore, and Sci-Fi Horror">

The violence in *Resident Alien* is a unique blend of slapstick comedy and genuine horror. Because the protagonist, Harry, is an alien who initially views humans as livestock or pests, his interactions can be quite brutal. The show does not shy away from showing the physical consequences of alien technology and superhuman strength.

Parents should be particularly mindful of the "autopsy" scenes. As Harry takes over the role of a town doctor, he frequently handles human organs and corpses. These scenes are played for laughs but feature realistic-looking prosthetic organs and blood.

| Type of Violence | Intensity | Key Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Graphic Gore** | **Moderate** | Severed feet, exposed organs during autopsies, and bloody lips. |
| **Physical Combat** | **High** | Neck-breaking, strangulation, and characters being thrown through walls. |
| **Threats to Children** | **Frequent** | Harry repeatedly threatens to kill a young boy (Max) who can see his true form. |
| **Domestic Abuse** | **Mild** | A character is shown with bruises and cuts from an abusive marriage. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Disturbing Imagery">
One of the most potentially upsetting recurring jokes involves Harry's mission to murder a child. While it is framed comedically, the visual of an alien looming over a bed with a surgical saw may be too intense for younger or sensitive children.
</Callout>

Specific instances of violence include Harry drowning the real Dr. Vanderspeigle in the pilot episode and a woman killing a man by breaking his neck in a later sequence. While the show isn't a "slasher," the "Moderate" violence tag from IMDb is well-earned.
